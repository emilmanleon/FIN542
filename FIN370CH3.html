Bank Deposit Accounts

Savings Account

A savings account is an interest-bearing deposit account held at a bank or other financial institution that provides a modest interest rate.

Key Features:

  • Interest-bearing account with typically lower rates than fixed deposits
  • Federally insured by PIDM for up to RM250,000
  • Convenient access to funds for everyday transactions
  • Minimum initial deposit requirement (RM20-RM1,000)
  • Available to individuals aged 18 and above (12-18 with parent/guardian approval)

Account Opening Procedure:

  1. 1

    Identification Verification - Provide valid identification (MyKad/passport)

  2. 2

    Complete Application Form - Fill and sign the savings account application form

  3. 3

    Initial Deposit - Deposit the minimum required amount

  4. 4

    Additional Documentation - For minors (12-18), parent/guardian must be present

Did You Know?

Most modern savings accounts come with a debit card and mobile banking access, allowing easy transfers and payments without visiting a branch.

Current Account

A current account is a type of bank account designed for frequent transactions, offering check facilities and no fixed placement period or transaction limits.

Key Features:

  • Cheque facilities for making payments
  • No fixed placement period or transaction limits
  • Access to bank overdraft services
  • Higher minimum initial deposit (RM500-RM1,000)
  • Minimum balance requirements to avoid charges

Understanding Cheques:

Cheque Components
  • • Date of issuance
  • • Payee's name
  • • Amount in words and figures
  • • Drawer's signature
  • • Crossings for security

Opening Requirements:

  • Valid identification (MyKad/passport)
  • Minimum initial deposit (RM500-RM1,000)
  • Some banks require an introducer (existing account holder)
  • Completed application form with signature
  • Minimum age of 18 years

Important Note:

An undischarged bankrupt cannot operate a current account, whether personal, joint, or partnership, except when acting as an agent. Bankrupts are also prohibited from acting as directors of limited companies.

Fixed Deposits

Fixed deposit accounts are investment accounts where customers deposit money for a predetermined period at a fixed interest rate, with guaranteed returns upon maturity.

Key Features:

  • Fixed term periods from 1 month to 60 months
  • Higher interest rates than savings accounts
  • Minimum deposit typically starting from RM500
  • Interest may be paid monthly, half-yearly, or at maturity
  • Early withdrawal may result in reduced or no interest payment

Negotiable Certificates of Deposit

The Ringgit Negotiable Instrument of Deposit (NID) is a transferable deposit placed for a specific period at an agreed interest rate. Unlike fixed deposits, it does not bear the holder's name and can be sold in the secondary market at any time.

Key Features:

  • Transferable financial instrument that can be traded in secondary markets
  • Does not bear any holder's name (unlike Fixed Deposits)
  • Can be converted to cash by selling to the secondary market
  • Only valid if issued by an institution authorized by Bank Negara Malaysia
  • Typically higher minimum investment amounts

Differences between NID and Fixed Deposit:

Feature Negotiable Certificate of Deposit Fixed Deposit
Transferability Freely transferable Not transferable
Holder Identification No name on certificate Name recorded on receipt
Liquidity Can be sold in secondary market Early withdrawal with penalty
Minimum Amount Typically higher As low as RM500
Target Market Institutional investors Retail customers

Important Considerations:

  • Market price of NCDs fluctuates with interest rate changes
  • Verify issuer is authorized by Bank Negara Malaysia
  • Understand secondary market liquidity conditions
  • May be subject to different tax treatment than regular deposits

Market Insight

NCDs are primarily used by larger businesses and institutional investors that require more flexibility than traditional fixed deposits. The secondary market for NCDs allows holders to exit investments before maturity, making them a more liquid option for organizations with changing cash flow needs.

Step 1: Identification & Eligibility

The bank verifies the identity of the applicant and determines their eligibility to open the specific type of account.

  • Verify MyKad, passport or other identification documents
  • Check age and contractual capacity of applicant
  • For business accounts, verify business registration with SSM
  • Screen for bankruptcies or other legal barriers

Step 2: Documentation Requirements

Different account types require specific supporting documents:

Account Type Required Documents
Individual MyKad/Passport, proof of address
Joint IDs for all parties, signed mandate with instructions
Trust Trust Deed, trustee IDs, specimen signatures
Companies Board resolution, M&A, officer IDs, mandate
Club/Society Constitution, meeting minutes, officer IDs

Step 3: Application Completion

The applicant completes and submits the account opening application and related forms:

  • Account opening form with all required information
  • Specimen signature form
  • Online/mobile banking registration (if applicable)
  • FATCA/CRS self-certification form
  • Debit card application (if applicable)

Step 4: Initial Deposit

The customer makes the minimum required deposit to activate the account.

Common Minimum Deposit Requirements:
  • Savings Account: RM20 - RM1,000
  • Current Account: RM500 - RM1,000
  • Fixed Deposit: RM500 - RM5,000
  • Club/Society Account: RM2,000

Payment can typically be made via cash, check, or electronic transfer, depending on the bank's policies.

Step 5: Account Activation & Setup

The bank completes final verification and activates the account:

  • Account number generation
  • Issuance of passbook/debit card/cheque book (as applicable)
  • Activation of online/mobile banking access
  • Provision of account terms and conditions documentation
  • Customer education on account features and services

Real-life Example: Joint Account Opening

Married couple John and Lisa decide to open a joint savings account at Hong Leong Bank to manage their household expenses. Here's how the process works:

  1. 1

    Initial Visit: Both John and Lisa visit the bank together with their MyKad IDs and proof of address.

  2. 2

    Documentation: They complete the joint account application form, specifying in the mandate that either of them can sign to withdraw funds.

  3. 3

    Survivorship Clause: They review and agree to the survivorship clause, which ensures that if one dies, the other automatically has full ownership of the account.

  4. 4

    Initial Deposit: They make an initial deposit of RM2,000 to open the account.

  5. 5

    Account Setup: They set up online banking access for both users and arrange for their respective salaries to be deposited into this account.

Benefits of Their Joint Account:

  • Simplified household financial management
  • Convenient access for both partners
  • Financial continuity in case one partner is unavailable
  • Protection through survivorship clause
  • PIDM protection up to RM250,000

Perbadanan Insurans Deposit Malaysia (PIDM)

PIDM is a Malaysian government agency that protects depositors against the loss of their insured deposits in the unlikely event a member bank or insurer fails.

Key Points

  • Protects deposits up to RM250,000 per depositor per member bank
  • Automatic coverage - no application or fee required
  • Covers conventional and Islamic deposits
  • Government-backed statutory body

What PIDM Protects

Deposits in Member Institutions

Savings, current, and fixed deposit accounts in PIDM member banks.

Foreign Currency Deposits

Deposits in foreign currencies are also protected and converted to Ringgit Malaysia.

Joint Accounts

Each joint account holder's share is insured separately up to RM250,000.

Trust Accounts

Each beneficiary's interest is protected separately up to RM250,000.

What PIDM Does Not Protect

Investment Products

Stocks, bonds, unit trusts, and other securities.

Deposits in Non-Member Institutions

Deposits in finance companies or institutions not listed as PIDM members.

Deposits Exceeding RM250,000

The portion of deposits that exceeds the RM250,000 limit per depositor per institution.

Digital Assets

Cryptocurrencies and other digital currencies are not protected by PIDM.

How PIDM Protection Works

During Normal Banking Operations

  1. 1

    Automatic Coverage: All depositors in PIDM member banks are automatically protected, with no application or fee required.

  2. 2

    Member Institutions: PIDM ensures member institutions adhere to sound financial practices and regulatory requirements.

  3. 3

    Monitoring: PIDM continuously monitors the financial health of its member institutions in coordination with Bank Negara Malaysia.

  4. 4

    Premium Collection: Member institutions pay annual premiums to PIDM based on their risk profiles and deposit base.

PIDM Member Institutions

All commercial banks operating in Malaysia are PIDM members, including:

  • • Maybank
  • • CIMB Bank
  • • Public Bank
  • • RHB Bank
  • • Hong Leong Bank
  • • AmBank
  • • And many more...

A full list of member institutions is available on the PIDM website.

In the Event of a Bank Failure

  1. 1

    Regulatory Action: Bank Negara Malaysia would first take regulatory actions to address issues at the troubled institution.

  2. 2

    PIDM Intervention: If the situation deteriorates, PIDM can intervene using various resolution tools, including assuming control of the failing institution.

  3. 3

    Resolution Options: PIDM might arrange a transfer of deposits to a healthy institution, establish a bridge institution, or provide financial assistance.

  4. 4

    Deposit Insurance Payout: If needed, PIDM would reimburse depositors up to the insured limit of RM250,000 per depositor per institution.

Important Note

Bank failures are extremely rare in Malaysia due to the strong regulatory framework overseen by Bank Negara Malaysia and PIDM's early intervention mechanisms.

The deposit insurance system aims to maintain public confidence in the banking system and protect small depositors from losses.

Claim Process for Depositors

  1. 1

    Public Notification: PIDM would make a public announcement through various media channels if a member institution fails.

  2. 2

    Depositor Verification: PIDM would verify depositor information using the failed institution's records.

  3. 3

    Payment Methods: Reimbursement could be made via various methods including direct deposit to a new account, cheques, or other payment mechanisms.

  4. 4

    Timing: PIDM aims to reimburse depositors as quickly as possible, typically within days or weeks of the failure.

  5. 5

    Uninsured Deposits: Depositors with amounts exceeding RM250,000 may receive partial recovery of the uninsured portion during the liquidation process.

Maximizing Your PIDM Protection

Tips for Better Protection

  • Spread deposits across multiple PIDM member banks if you have more than RM250,000.
  • Use different ownership categories at the same bank (individual, joint, trust accounts) as each category has separate insurance coverage.
  • Verify membership status of your bank with PIDM's official list of member institutions.
  • Keep records of your deposits, including account numbers and statements.
  • Stay informed about changes to PIDM policies and coverage limits.

Example: Multiple Ownership Categories

Ahmad has the following accounts at XYZ Bank:

  • Individual Savings Account: RM200,000
  • Joint Account with Wife: RM300,000 (RM150,000 each)
  • Trust Account for Child: RM100,000

Total PIDM Coverage: RM450,000

Each ownership category has its own RM250,000 protection limit.
